Child Protection Foundation launched its book and held a fundraiser event.

PROTECT THE CHILD FOUNDATION “BOOK/CLUB LAUNCH AND FUNDRAISER EVENT” HELD AT THE BANQUET HALL OF MERIT HOUSE MAITAMA ABUJA ON 24TH NOVEMBER 2022.

The Protect the Child Foundation on Thursday 24th November ,2022 launched her book titled “The Trainers Manual: A handbook on how to prevent, respond and report cases of child sexual abuse” and Protect The Child Foundation Clubs in 5 schools within Federal Capital Territory, Abuja, Nigeria namely Junior Secondary School Lower Usman Dam, Bwari Area Council, Junior Secondary School Kpegyi, Junior Secondary School Kurudu, Junior Secondary School Nyanya II, Local Education Authority (LEA) Nyanya II all within FCT at the Banquet Hall of the Merit and Awards House Maitama, Abuja. Although the clubs in schools commenced in September, it was officially launched in November, 2023.

Protect The Child Foundation (PTCF) a Non-Governmental and non-profit Organization committed to the protection and defense of the innocence of children against all forms of sexual abuse and exploitation. PTCF has a vision of creating a safe childhood free from sexual abuse for the African Child.

“The Trainers Manual: A handbook on how to prevent, respond and report cases of child sexual abuse” is aimed at arming teachers, parents, caregivers, and relevant stakeholders with important and useful information and encourage them to have conversations around body safety, age appropriate sex education and channels of reportage of child sexual abuse. The Trainers Manual is a book comprising of 9 modules covering issues around medical perspective of signs and symptoms of an abused child, understanding the concept of sexual grooming, age appropriate sex/sexuality education, important laws on child abuse in Nigeria and how to get justice for survivors of child sexual abuse, interviewing and counselling of Survivors of Child sexual abuse. The book also contains lists of appropriate agencies to report sexual abuse in Nigeria, global care lines and their contacts and more. Just like Mr. Clement Chukwuemeka Esq. the 2nd Vice President of the Nigerian Bar Association one of the book reviewers aptly puts it during the book review “The book is a classic, collective research and findings of medical doctors, lawyers of repute, journalists of impeccable character, law enforcement agents of wonderful character, civil society activists amongst others….the book had been published to bridge the gap and create the needed awareness towards eradicating child sexual abuse in our society”.

The PTCF Club which was also launched is part of the foundation’s program to keep in touch with school children both boys and girls, guide them on how to prevent, respond and report any form of abuse done on them. They are also taught basic life skills, leadership, self-esteem and are engaged in character building programs. The PTCF club now holds activities in five (5) schools within the FCT with over 80 students in each of the clubs. The Foundation wishes to reach more schools and states in Nigeria with her club activities so as to impact more lives come 2023.

The Executive Director of the PTCF Elizabeth Ebulejonu Achimugu Esq. in her welcome address acquainted the audience on how the Foundation came about. “in 2017, after I had my first child I was beginning to wander sexual safety most times people talk about just for girls but I know boys too can be abused. So what can we do about these things, I realized people don’t like to talk about it when it comes up people just want to push it aside and that is what gave birth to Protect The Child Foundation.

She expressed her gratitude to everyone for being present and hope they would see the need to join hands with the PTCF to ensure a safe society for all children in Nigeria and Africa at large.

The book launched had 4 book reviewers from different agencies namely: Mr. Clement Chukwuemeka Esq. the 2nd Vice President of the Nigerian Bar Association, Hajia Rabi Usman Awobajo of Nature Care Therapeutic Services, Mrs Helen Emuze the representative of the Director General of NAPTIP, and Jamila Abdul the representative of PsT. Regina Omo-Ageege PhD of Women Education Advocacy and Development Initiative.

The event saw the presence of some important personnel in both public and private agencies like the National Agency for the Prohibition of Trafficking in Persons (NAPTIP), Police, National Human Rights Commission, Nigerian Bar Association (NBA), Centre for Journalism Innovation & Development (CJID), other NGOs such as Women’s Rights Advancement and Protection Alternative (WRAPA), Roxanna Ozoemena Foundation, Cece Yara Foundation, schools and many more.

The production of the PTCF book and club activities have been launched with the support of Facebook Community Accelerator, 2021.
